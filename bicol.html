Bicol Express

Bicol Express is a fiery Filipino stew made with succulent pork, coconut milk, and a generous amount of chili peppers. Named after the passenger train service from Manila to the Bicol region, it is famous for its rich, creamy texture and intense heat.

Ingredients:

  • 500g Pork belly, cut into small cubes
  • 2 cups Coconut milk (gata)
  • 1 cup Coconut cream (kakang gata)
  • 1/2 cup Shrimp paste (bagoong alamang)
  • 1 cup Siling haba (long green chilies), sliced
  • 2-3 pieces Siling labuyo (bird's eye chili), minced for extra heat
  • 1 head Garlic, minced
  • 1 medium Onion, chopped
  • 1 tbsp Ginger, minced
  • 1 tbsp Cooking oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Step by Step Guide

  1. Heat cooking oil in a pan over medium heat and sauté the ginger, garlic, and onions until fragrant.
  2. Add the pork belly and cook until the meat is lightly browned and the fat begins to render.
  3. Stir in the shrimp paste (bagoong) and cook for another 2 minutes to release its aroma.
  4. Pour in the coconut milk and bring to a gentle simmer. Lower the heat and let it cook until the pork is tender.
  5. Add the sliced green chilies and bird's eye chilies. Stir well to incorporate the heat into the sauce.
  6. Pour in the thick coconut cream (kakang gata) and continue to simmer until the sauce thickens and becomes oily.
  7. Season with salt and pepper if needed, bearing in mind that the shrimp paste is already salty.
  8. Serve hot with plenty of steamed white rice to help balance the spiciness.
