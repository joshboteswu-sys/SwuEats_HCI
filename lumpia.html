Lumpiang Shanghai

Lumpiang Shanghai is a popular Filipino appetizer consisting of ground pork and minced vegetables rolled in a thin crepe-like wrapper. These golden, deep-fried spring rolls are a staple at every Filipino gathering, loved for their addictive crunch and savory filling.

Ingredients:

  • 500g Ground pork
  • 1/2 cup Minced carrots
  • 1/2 cup Minced onions
  • 1/4 cup Minced garlic
  • 1/4 cup Minced spring onions
  • 1 large Egg
  • 1 tsp Salt
  • 1/2 tsp Ground black pepper
  • 1 packet Spring roll wrappers
  • Cooking oil

Step by Step Guide

  1. In a large bowl, combine ground pork, carrots, onions, garlic, and spring onions.
  2. Add the egg, salt, and pepper to the mixture. Stir thoroughly until well combined.
  3. Place a spoonful of the filling on a wrapper. Fold the sides in and roll tightly, sealing the edge with a bit of water or egg white.
  4. Repeat the process until all the filling is used. You can cut long rolls into smaller pieces if desired.
  5. Heat cooking oil in a pan over medium heat. Ensure there is enough oil to submerge the rolls.
  6. Fry the lumpia in batches until the wrapper is golden brown and the meat is fully cooked (about 3 to 5 minutes).
  7. Remove from the pan and drain excess oil on a wire rack or paper towels.
  8. Serve hot with sweet chili sauce or spiced vinegar.
