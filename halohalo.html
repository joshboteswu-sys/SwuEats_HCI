Halo-Halo

Halo-halo is the ultimate Filipino dessert, a vibrant explosion of textures and flavors layered in a tall glass. Its name literally translates to "mix-mix," which is exactly how you eat it—stirring the sweetened beans, fruits, and jellies into a slush of shaved milk and ice.

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup Shaved ice
  • 1/4 cup Evaporated milk
  • 1 scoop Ube ice cream
  • 1 slice Leche flan
  • 2 tbsp Sweetened mung beans (monggo)
  • 2 tbsp Sweetened kidney beans
  • 2 tbsp Macapuno strings (coconut sport)
  • 2 tbsp Nata de coco (coconut gel)
  • 2 tbsp Kaong (sugar palm fruit)
  • 2 tbsp Sweetened saba banana or sweet potato (kamote)
  • 1 tbsp Pinipig (toasted pounded rice)
  • 1 tbsp Ube halaya (purple yam jam)

Step by Step Guide

  1. In a tall glass, layer the bottom with the sweetened beans, macapuno, nata de coco, kaong, and bananas.
  2. Fill the glass about three-quarters full with finely shaved ice.
  3. Pour the evaporated milk over the ice, allowing it to soak through the layers.
  4. Top the ice with a generous slice of leche flan and a dollop of ube halaya.
  5. Add a scoop of ube ice cream on the very top for extra creaminess.
  6. Sprinkle with pinipig for a crunchy finish.
  7. Serve immediately with a long spoon; mix everything thoroughly before eating.
