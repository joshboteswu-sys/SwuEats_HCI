Pork Sisig

Pork Sisig is a celebrated Filipino dish made from chopped pig’s face, ears, and belly, seasoned with calamansi, onions, and chili peppers. It is the ultimate "pulutan" (beer snack) or main course, famous for its smoky, savory, and spicy profile served on a sizzling plate.

Ingredients:

  • 500g Pork belly and pork ears (boiled until tender)
  • 1/2 cup Chicken liver, grilled and chopped
  • 1 medium Red onion, finely minced
  • 3-5 pieces Bird's eye chili (siling labuyo), minced
  • 3 tbsp Calamansi juice
  • 2 tbsp Soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup Mayonnaise (optional, for creaminess)
  • 1 tsp Salt and 1/2 tsp black pepper
  • 2 tbsp Butter or margarine
  • 1 Egg (optional, for topping)

Step by Step Guide

  1. Grill or pan-fry the boiled pork until the skin is crisp and charred, then chop into very small pieces.
  2. In a large bowl, combine the chopped pork, chopped chicken liver, onions, and chilies.
  3. Add the calamansi juice, soy sauce, salt, and pepper. Toss until the meat is evenly coated.
  4. Heat a cast-iron sizzling plate or a heavy skillet over medium-high heat.
  5. Melt the butter or margarine on the hot plate.
  6. Add the sisig mixture to the plate and stir-fry for 3 to 5 minutes until the fat starts to render and sizzle.
  7. Stir in the mayonnaise if you prefer a creamier texture.
  8. Create a small well in the center, crack an egg into it, and serve immediately while still smoking.
