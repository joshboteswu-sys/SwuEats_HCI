Sinigang na Hipon

Sinigang na Hipon is a light and refreshing Filipino sour soup featuring succulent shrimp as the main protein. It is a popular alternative to the pork version, favored for its quick cooking time and clean, briny flavor that perfectly complements the tamarind base.

Ingredients:

  • 500g Large shrimp (head and shell on for better flavor)
  • 1 pack Tamarind (sinigang) mix
  • 1 medium Radish (labanos), sliced
  • 1 bunch Long beans (sitaw), cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 1 bunch Water spinach (kangkong)
  • 2 medium Tomatoes, quartered
  • 1 medium Onion, quartered
  • 2-3 pieces Long green chili (siling haba)
  • 1 tbsp Fish sauce (patis)
  • 4-5 cups Water

Step by Step Guide

  1. In a large pot, bring water to a boil with the onions and tomatoes until they soften slightly.
  2. Add the sliced radish and long beans, simmering for about 3 to 5 minutes until they are halfway cooked.
  3. Stir in the tamarind mix and fish sauce, adjusting the amount of mix to your preferred level of sourness.
  4. Add the shrimp and the long green chilies. Cook for only 2 to 3 minutes; avoid overcooking the shrimp so they stay tender and juicy.
  5. Taste the broth and add more fish sauce or salt if needed.
  6. Add the kangkong (water spinach) on top and immediately turn off the heat.
  7. Cover the pot for 1 minute to allow the residual heat to wilt the greens perfectly.
  8. Serve hot in a large bowl, ideally with a side of steamed white rice.
