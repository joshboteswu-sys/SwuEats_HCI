Chicken Tinola

Chicken Tinola is a soothing Filipino ginger-based soup. It is a light yet flavorful dish often served during rainy days or to someone feeling under the weather for its warming, restorative properties.

Ingredients:

  • 500g Chicken, cut into serving pieces
  • 1 small Green Papaya or Sayote, wedged
  • 1 cup Malunggay leaves or Chili leaves
  • 2 tbsp Ginger, sliced into strips
  • 1 medium Onion, chopped
  • 4 cloves Garlic, minced
  • 2 tbsp Fish sauce (Patis)
  • 4 cups Water or Rice wash
  • 1 tsp Whole peppercorns
  • 2 tbsp Cooking oil

Step by Step Guide

  1. Heat cooking oil in a pot over medium heat and sauté the ginger, garlic, and onions until fragrant.
  2. Add the chicken pieces and cook until the outer layer turns light brown.
  3. Pour in the fish sauce and peppercorns, stirring for 2 minutes to let the flavors penetrate the meat.
  4. Add the water or rice wash and bring to a boil.
  5. Lower the heat and simmer for about 20 minutes or until the chicken is tender.
  6. Add the green papaya or sayote wedges and cook for another 5 to 7 minutes until softened.
  7. Stir in the malunggay or chili leaves and turn off the heat.
  8. Cover the pot for 2 minutes to allow the leaves to wilt in the residual heat before serving hot.
