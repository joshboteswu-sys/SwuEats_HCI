Pinakbet

Pinakbet (or Pakbet) is a classic Filipino vegetable stew flavored with fermented shrimp paste. Originating from the Ilocos region, the name comes from the Ilokano word pinakebbet, meaning "shriveled," referring to the way the vegetables are cooked until they are tender and shrunk into the savory sauce.

Ingredients:

  • 250g Pork belly, sliced into small strips
  • 1/4 cup Shrimp paste (bagoong alamang)
  • 1 large Eggplant, sliced
  • 1 medium Bitter melon (ampalaya), deseeded and sliced
  • 1 cup Squash (kalabasa), cubed
  • 6-8 pieces Okra
  • 1 bunch Long beans (sitaw), cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 1 medium Onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves Garlic, minced
  • 2 medium Tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 cup Water or rice wash
  • 1 tbsp Cooking oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Step by Step Guide

  1. In a large pan or pot, heat the cooking oil and sauté the garlic, onions, and tomatoes until the tomatoes are soft.
  2. Add the pork belly strips and cook until they turn light brown and the fat begins to render.
  3. Stir in the shrimp paste (bagoong) and cook for 2 minutes to incorporate the flavor into the meat.
  4. Add the squash (kalabasa) and pour in the water. Cover and simmer for 5 minutes until the squash is slightly tender.
  5. Add the remaining vegetables: long beans, okra, eggplant, and bitter melon.
  6. Toss gently to coat the vegetables in the sauce, then cover the pot.
  7. Lower the heat and simmer for 8 to 10 minutes. Avoid over-stirring the bitter melon to prevent excessive bitterness from spreading.
  8. Cook until the vegetables are tender and the liquid has reduced. Season with salt and pepper if necessary.
  9. Serve hot as a side dish or a main course with steamed white rice.
