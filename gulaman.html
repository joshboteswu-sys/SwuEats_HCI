Sago't Gulaman

Sago't Gulaman is a refreshing Filipino cold drink made with brown sugar syrup, gelatin, and chewy pearls. It’s the ultimate street-side cooler, often served with plenty of crushed ice.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup brown sugar
  • 1 cup water
  • 1 tsp vanilla or pandan extract
  • 1 bar (or sachet) red or green agar-agar, cooked and cubed.
  • 1 cup cooked large tapioca pearls
  • Cold water
  • Ice

Step by Step Guide

  1. Arnibal: Simmer sugar and water until the sugar dissolves and slightly thickens. Stir in vanilla/pandan and let cool.
  2. Gulaman: Prepare agar-agar according to package instructions (use slightly less water for a firmer texture). Let it set, then cut into small cubes.
  3. Sago: Boil tapioca pearls until translucent. Rinse in cold water to prevent sticking.
  4. Combine: In a large pitcher or individual glasses, combine the syrup, gulaman cubes, and sago.
  5. Serve: Add cold water to reach your desired sweetness and top with lots of ice.
